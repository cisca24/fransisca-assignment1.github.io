Project Details

Introduction

The "Land Subsidence Study" project aims to comprehensively analyze the causes, impacts, and mitigation strategies related to land subsidence, focusing on urban areas affected by groundwater extraction and natural compaction.

Objectives

  • Investigate the primary causes of land subsidence, including groundwater depletion and soil compaction, using advanced geospatial technologies.
  • Analyze historical data and satellite imagery to detect and monitor subsidence trends over time.
  • Evaluate the socioeconomic and environmental impacts of subsidence on infrastructure, communities, and natural ecosystems.
  • Develop predictive models to forecast future subsidence scenarios and assess their potential consequences.

Methodology

The study utilizes Geographic Information Systems (GIS), remote sensing techniques, and Global Navigation Satellite System (GNSS) data for spatial analysis and monitoring. Field surveys and sensor networks complement satellite observations, providing a holistic understanding of subsidence processes.

Results and Findings

Preliminary findings indicate significant subsidence rates in urban centers due to intensive groundwater extraction, leading to infrastructure damage and increased flood risks. Spatial analysis reveals hotspots vulnerable to future subsidence, guiding targeted mitigation efforts.

Recommendations

Based on the study's findings, recommendations include implementing sustainable groundwater management practices, integrating subsidence risk assessments into urban planning frameworks, and raising public awareness to mitigate subsidence-related risks effectively.
